Who we serve

One national platform for every part of the community

EdGen is built to serve families, donors, schools, and communities — all through a single, full-service Scholarship Granting Organization.

Families

Jewish K–12 families in opted-in states whose household income falls within the 300% AMI threshold. EdGen handles your application, income verification, and scholarship award — at no cost to you.

Donors

Individuals who contribute to EdGen receive a 100% federal tax credit — up to $1,700 for single filers, $3,400 for married filing jointly. Your entire contribution goes directly to scholarship awards.

Schools

Yeshivos, Bais Yaakovs, and Jewish day schools in participating states can partner with EdGen to support their students. We handle the compliance, reporting, and scholarship disbursement.

Communities

Federations, kehillos, and communal organizations can partner with EdGen to bring FSTC access to their communities — through locally branded campaigns, outreach support, and program education.

How it works

From application to scholarship in three steps

EdGen manages the full scholarship pipeline — so families can focus on their children, donors can focus on their impact, and schools can focus on their students.

Step 1

Families Apply

Parents submit an application through EdGen's secure portal, providing household income information, student details, school enrollment, and authorization for income verification. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis.

Step 2

Eligibility Is Verified

EdGen verifies income through the IRS Income Verification Express Service (IVES), confirms school eligibility and state participation, and determines scholarship award amounts based on program funding and household need.

Step 3

Scholarships Are Awarded

Scholarship funds are disbursed directly to eligible schools on behalf of qualified families. Donors receive tax credit documentation. Families receive award confirmation and enrollment support from EdGen's team.
