The basics

What is the Federal Scholarship Tax Credit?

The Federal Scholarship Tax Credit (FSTC) is a federal program enacted in 2025 that provides a dollar-for-dollar tax credit — not a deduction, but a full credit — for contributions made to eligible Scholarship Granting Organizations (SGOs). Donors receive a 100% credit against their federal income tax liability, up to $1,700 for single filers and $3,400 for married taxpayers filing jointly (subject to final Treasury/IRS guidance).

SGOs use these contributions to award scholarships to eligible K–12 students attending private schools in participating states. Eligibility is based on household income — specifically, households whose income falls at or below 300% of the Area Median Income (AMI) for their county.

EdGen is a 501(c)(3) SGO focused exclusively on serving Orthodox Jewish families — connecting donors who want 100% credit for their giving with families who need scholarship support for yeshivos, Bais Yaakovs, and Jewish day schools.

What scholarships can cover

Eligible Education Expenses

FSTC scholarships can be applied to a range of qualified K–12 education expenses. Final guidance from the IRS and Treasury on the full scope of eligible expenses is pending.

Tuition & Fees

Annual and semester tuition charged by eligible K–12 private schools, including registration fees, activity fees, and mandatory school charges.

Books & Supplies

Required textbooks, workbooks, Judaic studies materials, lab supplies, and other educational materials required for enrolled courses.

Other Approved Expenses

Additional qualified K–12 education expenses as defined under the program. The full list of eligible expense categories is subject to final IRS/Treasury rulemaking.

Pending guidance: The complete list of eligible expenses — including whether transportation, uniforms, tutoring, or other costs qualify — awaits final IRS/Treasury rules. Who EdGen serves

For Families, Donors, and Schools

Families: How to apply

If your household income is at or below 300% of the Area Median Income for your county, and your child attends (or will attend) an eligible private K–12 school in a participating state, you may qualify for an EdGen scholarship.

EdGen's application process is designed to be clear, secure, and free to families. We handle the verification process so you can focus on what matters — your child's education.

Donors: Make an impact at zero net cost

When you contribute to EdGen, you receive a 100% federal tax credit — up to $1,700 if you file single, or up to $3,400 if you file married filing jointly (subject to final Treasury/IRS guidance). That means your contribution results in $0 net out of pocket after your tax credit.

100% of your contribution goes directly to scholarship awards for eligible students. EdGen charges 0% administrative fees — our overhead is covered separately, so every dollar you give reaches a family.

You may optionally designate your contribution toward a specific school, school system, city, or state — subject to IRS rules prohibiting earmarks for specific students.

Program FAQ

Families in states that have not opted in are not currently eligible for FSTC scholarships from EdGen. If your child attends school in a different state than your home state, the rules regarding which state determines eligibility await final IRS/Treasury guidance.

The Area Median Income (AMI) is set annually by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) at the county or metropolitan statistical area level. For FSTC eligibility, a household's income must fall at or below 300% of the applicable AMI. The eligibility threshold is a single 300% AMI figure and does not adjust by household size. The map on this page shows the 2026 HUD 300% AMI threshold for a 4-person household by county.

IVES stands for the IRS Income Verification Express Service — a program that allows authorized third parties to retrieve IRS tax transcripts on behalf of taxpayers who provide consent. EdGen uses IVES to verify household income in a secure, official manner, without requiring families to submit sensitive financial documents directly. IVES integration is not active during the pre-launch phase.
