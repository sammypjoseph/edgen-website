About EdGen

Built to Serve Every Jewish Family

EdGen is a new 501(c)(3) Scholarship Granting Organization founded to make Jewish day school education accessible to every eligible family through the Federal Scholarship Tax Credit.

Our mission

What makes EdGen different

EdGen is the only national Scholarship Granting Organization built specifically for Orthodox Jewish families. We exist to close the gap between the families who need school choice and the donors who want to make a difference — with zero administrative friction and zero cost passed to students.

Vision & Mission

Why we exist

Our Vision

A future where every Jewish family can send their child to the school that is best for them, regardless of financial circumstances.

Our Mission

EdGen exists to make Jewish day school accessible to every eligible family by operating a full-service, 0% fee Scholarship Granting Organization for the Federal Scholarship Tax Credit — connecting donors who want to make a difference with the children and schools that need their support.

Core values

What we stand for

Every Family Deserves a Choice

School choice is not a privilege — it is a fundamental right for Jewish families. We exist to make it real, accessible, and affordable for every eligible household.

Transparency Over Everything

0% fees, clear eligibility rules, honest communication about pending guidance. EdGen never overpromises. We tell families, donors, and schools exactly what we know and what we don't.

Communal Responsibility

Jewish education is a communal obligation — not just an individual family's burden. EdGen mobilizes donors, schools, and communities to share the cost and the credit.

Excellence in Execution

The Orthodox Jewish community deserves an SGO with the operational rigor, compliance discipline, and customer service of a best-in-class organization. We build for that standard.

Respect for Every Student

Scholarships are not charity — they are a mechanism for families to access what their children deserve. We treat every applicant with the dignity and respect that entails.

Long-Term Thinking

EdGen is being built to last — with the governance, infrastructure, and relationships to serve Jewish families not just at launch, but for generations.

Our story

How EdGen came to be

When Congress enacted the Federal Scholarship Tax Credit as part of the Tax Cuts & Jobs Act of 2025, it created something the Jewish community had never seen before: a federal mechanism that would allow donors to give up to $3,400 per year to scholarship organizations (subject to final Treasury/IRS guidance) — and receive a full, 100% tax credit for every dollar.

The opportunity was historic. But the Orthodox Jewish community needed a purpose-built SGO — one that understood yeshivos, Bais Yaakovs, and Jewish day schools; one that could serve families in 29 states simultaneously; one that would charge 0% administrative fees and put every donated dollar into scholarships.

EdGen was founded to be that organization. Named for Education Generation — the idea that every generation of Jewish children deserves the full support of their community — EdGen is being built from the ground up to serve the FSTC's full potential for Orthodox Jewish families.

We are a 501(c)(3) organization, pre-launch, with a program launch target of January 1, 2027. We have the endorsement of four major national Jewish organizations — Jewish Federations of North America, Torah Umesorah, the Orthodox Union, and Prizmah — and we are building the systems, relationships, and team to deliver on this mission.

"The FSTC is a once-in-a-generation opportunity for Jewish education. EdGen exists to make sure no eligible family is left behind."
— EdGen Founding Team
